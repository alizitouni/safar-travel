Overview

In the Footsteps of Al-Andalus

For eight centuries, the Muslim civilisation of Al-Andalus produced some of history's greatest achievements in art, science, philosophy, and architecture. Today, southern Spain — Andalusia — preserves this magnificent legacy in its palaces, mosques-turned-cathedrals, and the very streets of its ancient medinas. Walking through the Alhambra or the Great Mosque of Córdoba is a profound experience for any Muslim traveller.

This journey connects you with that history while surrounding you with authentic Andalusian luxury — parador hotels in historic buildings, private guided access before tourist crowds, halal dining in cities where the food culture perfectly aligns with Islamic values, and an evening of flamenco — the passionate art form born in this very land.

Day by Day

Your Itinerary

Day 1–2
Arrival in Seville
Fly into Seville and settle into your luxury hotel in the historic Barrio de Santa Cruz. Private guided tour of the magnificent Real Alcázar — a Moorish palace that UNESCO calls one of the finest examples of Mudéjar architecture in the world — and the vast Gothic cathedral built on the site of a former mosque. Evening tapas dinner at a halal-certified traditional restaurant, joining the Sevillano tradition of eating late under warm southern skies.
Real AlcázarSeville CathedralHalal tapas dinner
Day 3
Flamenco & Seville by Night
Morning at leisure to explore the atmospheric Santa Cruz quarter — white-washed lanes, orange trees, hidden plazas. Afternoon boat cruise on the Guadalquivir River. Evening at an intimate flamenco performance in a historic venue — the pure, passionate art form born in Andalusia, experiencing it as a live spectacle rather than a tourist show.
Santa Cruz quarterRiver cruiseFlamenco show
Day 4
Córdoba — The Great Mosque
Drive to Córdoba, once the greatest city in medieval Europe and the capital of Islamic Spain. The Mezquita-Catedral — the Great Mosque with 856 columns and its famous forest of red-and-white striped arches — is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the most extraordinary buildings in the world. Private guided visit including the Jewish Quarter and the Roman Bridge. Return to Seville for dinner.
La MezquitaJewish QuarterRoman Bridge
Day 5–6
Granada — The Alhambra
Drive to Granada, set against the backdrop of the snow-capped Sierra Nevada. Private early-morning access to the Alhambra Palace — the last great fortress of Islamic Spain — before the crowds arrive. Wander through the Nasrid Palaces with their intricate geometric tilework and arabesques, the Generalife gardens, and the Alcazaba fortress. Afternoon exploration of the Albaicín — the old Moorish quarter, a labyrinth of white houses and tea houses still carrying the scent of Al-Andalus.
Alhambra PalaceNasrid PalacesGeneralife GardensAlbaicín quarter
Day 7
Sierra Nevada & White Villages
Day excursion into the Sierra Nevada foothills and the famous White Villages — Alpujarras pueblos that retain their Moorish character to this day. Visit Pampaneira, Bubión, and Capileira perched on the mountainside. Local halal lunch at a traditional posada. Return to Granada for a final evening in the city.
White VillagesSierra NevadaMoorish villages
Day 8–9
Farewell & Departure
Final morning in Granada — perhaps a last tea in the Albaicín at a Moorish teahouse, reflecting on eight days in the land of Al-Andalus. Private transfer to Granada or Málaga airport for your departure flight.
