Overview

Southeast Asia's Muslim Paradise

Malaysia is arguably the world's best destination for Muslim travellers. With over 60% of the population Muslim, halal food is not a speciality — it is simply the norm. Mosques are beautiful and everywhere. The culture is deeply Islamic yet completely cosmopolitan, blending Malay, Chinese, and Indian influences into one of the world's most fascinating and delicious food cultures.

This 10-night journey moves from the futuristic skyline of Kuala Lumpur to the lush rainforest and pristine beaches of Langkawi, ending in the food paradise of Penang. Extraordinary for families — children are welcomed everywhere, the activities are endlessly varied, and the country is supremely safe and easy to navigate.

Day by Day

Your Itinerary

Day 1–3
Kuala Lumpur — City of Towers
Arrive in Kuala Lumpur and check into your luxury city hotel. Private guided city tour: the iconic Petronas Twin Towers with VIP Sky Bridge access, the ornate Masjid Jamek mosque at the confluence of two rivers, the vibrant Batu Caves Hindu temple (fascinating cultural contrast), and the colourful streets of Chinatown. Full day dedicated to Malaysia's food culture — private halal food tour through KL's legendary hawker centres and upscale halal restaurants, sampling nasi lemak, satay, laksa, and roti canai with an expert guide.
Petronas Towers VIPMasjid JamekHalal food tourBatu Caves
Day 4–7
Langkawi — Jungle & Beaches
Fly to Langkawi — a duty-free island archipelago of 99 islands coated in ancient rainforest and fringed by turquoise sea. Check into a luxury jungle or beachfront resort. Island hopping by private boat to the most beautiful coves and deserted beaches. Cable car to the top of Mount Mat Cincang for panoramic views across the archipelago. Mangrove kayaking through ancient jungle rivers. Snorkelling in crystal-clear waters. Malaysian cooking class preparing traditional halal dishes.
Island hoppingCable carMangrove kayakingCooking class
Day 8–10
Penang — Food Capital of Asia
Ferry or fly to Penang — a UNESCO-listed island city often called the food capital of Asia. The historic core of George Town is a labyrinth of Chinese shophouses, colonial buildings, and street art. Private halal food tour with a local guide — char kway teow, assam laksa, nasi kandar — visiting the legendary hawker stalls that have been cooking the same dishes for generations. Penang Hill funicular for panoramic island views. Day trip to Penang National Park for jungle trekking and secluded beach.
George Town UNESCOHalal food tourPenang HillNational Park
Day 11
Departure
Final morning in Penang — last breakfast of roti canai and teh tarik at a legendary mamak restaurant before private transfer to Penang International Airport for your departure flight. Malaysia will stay with you long after you leave.
